Gemini DTB boundary
-------------------

The decompiled `mt6797-gemini-pda.dtb` contains two CCCI reserved-memory
nodes, both `no-map`:

  reserve-memory-ccci_md1: size 0xa100000, alignment 0x2000000,
    alloc-ranges 0x40000000..0xc0000000
  reserve-memory-ccci_share: size 0x600000, alignment 0x4000000

It contains zero matches for active `mdcldma`, `ap2c2k_ccif`, `ccif_modem`,
`cldma_modem`, or `ccci_modem` transport nodes. The board patch adds only the
two reservation nodes. Thus the package preserves firmware/modem carve-outs
without attempting to own the modem transport.

Decision
--------

The current package can provide generic WWAN port infrastructure, but it has
no Gemini modem transport. Linux `t7xx` remains unselected because it is the
PCIe/DPMAIF T700 backend; MHI helpers are not a match for APB CCCI. A future
MT6797 driver must own the CCCI header, CLDMA/CCIF rings, channel map,
firmware handshake, reset, shared-memory lifetime, clocks, and EMI-MPU policy,
then expose standard WWAN/TTY/netdev interfaces above that transport. Keep the
two reservations intact and the modem transport nodes absent until those
ownership contracts are recovered.

runtime_mainline_boot=not_attempted
runtime_modem_probe=not_attempted
firmware_handshake=not_attempted
