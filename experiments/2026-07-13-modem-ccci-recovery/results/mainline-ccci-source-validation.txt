Both commands passed. The analyzer confirms that the live Gemini has separate
MD1 CCCI and MD3/C2K CCCI domains, while the vendor MT6797 transport uses
APB-mapped CLDMA/CCIF windows, vendor clocks, bootloader-owned shared memory,
and EMI MPU/remap policy. Linux 7.1.3 `t7xx` is a PCIe/DPMAIF T700 driver and is
not a transport match.

Decision: do not reuse `t7xx` as a Gemini modem driver. Add a new MT6797
CCCI/CLDMA transport/backend only after shared-memory, reset/handshake, and EMI
MPU ownership are recovered; reuse standard WWAN/TTY/netdev interfaces above
that transport.

WWAN reuse boundary:
  `include/linux/wwan.h` exposes `wwan_create_port`, `wwan_remove_port`,
  `wwan_port_rx`, `wwan_port_txoff`/`wwan_port_txon`, and
  `wwan_register_ops`; a transport supplies `wwan_port_ops` start/stop/tx
  callbacks and a `wwan_port_caps` framing description. The generic core
  exposes AT/MBIM/QMI/QCDM/FIREHOSE/XMMRPC/FASTBOOT/ADB/MIPC/NMEA ports,
  while a modem-specific backend owns only channel mapping, queueing,
  lifecycle, and protocol framing.
  `rpmsg_wwan_ctrl` is a concrete adapter pattern: it receives on its lower
  endpoint and calls `wwan_port_rx`, then creates a port with
  `wwan_create_port`. MHI and USB QMI/MBIM drivers demonstrate other lower
  layers, but their MHI/USB dependencies do not match the APB CCCI device. A
  future MT6797 CCCI backend should follow this WWAN port boundary and add a
  normal netdev for `ccmni` data, without exposing vendor `/dev/ccci*` or
  ioctl ABI.

Linux 7.1.3 object validation:
  `wwan_core.o` and `rpmsg_wwan_ctrl.o` compile in the prepared arm64 VM
  output tree. `t7xx_hif_cldma.o`, `t7xx_port_proxy.o`, and `t7xx_netdev.o`
  also compile for source comparison; this validates build contracts only and
  does not imply that the PCIe/DPMAIF driver can probe Gemini.

Normalized contract assertions also pass: 16-byte CCCI header; 8+8 CLDMA and
CCIF queue families; packed 16-byte TGPD/RGPD/TBD/RBD descriptors with 36-bit
address metadata; MD1/MD3 port ranges; six DT windows; and staged EMI-MPU
regions 11/14/16/17. The complete sanitized design record is
`results/mt6797-ccci-mainline-contract.md`.
