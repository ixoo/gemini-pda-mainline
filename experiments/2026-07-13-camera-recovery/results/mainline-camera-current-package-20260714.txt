The package contains generic media/codec modules (`imx219.ko`, `imx412.ko`,
`mtk_jpeg.ko`, `mtk-mdp3.ko`, and `mtk-vcodec-dec.ko`) but no `ov5675.ko` or
SP5509 module. These generic modules are not evidence of a Gemini camera
consumer or a matching sensor.

Device-tree boundary
--------------------

The Gemini DTB has zero capture-node matches for `seninf`, `camsv`, `camtop`,
`cama`, `camb`, `dip_a`, camera, or ISP nodes. It retains two clock-provider
syscon nodes (`mt6797-imgsys` at `0x15000000` and `mt6797-camsys` at
`0x1a000000`), but the camera SMI common node and both camera/image larbs are
`status = "disabled"`. No sensor, endpoint, receiver, media graph, or camera
ISP node is present in the packaged board DTB.

Decision
--------

The package provides standard media-controller and generic MediaTek media
building blocks, plus the MT6797 CAMSYS clock provider, but no Gemini sensor
or capture pipeline. Linux's OV5675 driver is correctly not selected, and
SP5509 has no existing driver or Kconfig symbol. A future implementation must
add a new SP5509 V4L2 sub-device and a separately recovered MT6797 SENINF/
CSI/capture pipeline; it must not alias OV5675 or enable the generic codec
modules as a camera path. Keep the SMI/larb and camera consumers disabled until
sensor address, power/reset, MIPI endpoint, receiver registers, and DMA/IOMMU
ownership are independently established.
