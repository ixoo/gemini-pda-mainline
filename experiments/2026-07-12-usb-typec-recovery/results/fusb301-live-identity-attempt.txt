FUSB301 live identity/read-only access attempt
===============================================

The running device exposes two already-bound FUSB301-family I2C clients, but
the vendor kernel does not expose an I2C character device or a register-dump
sysfs/debugfs attribute to an unprivileged collector.

device=gemini@192.168.1.50
kernel=3.18.41+
client0=/sys/bus/i2c/devices/0-0025 name=fusb301a driver=FUSB301_1
client1=/sys/bus/i2c/devices/1-0025 name=fusb301 driver=FUSB301_0
device_nodes=/dev/i2c*=absent
sysfs_register_attributes=none
debugfs_fusb_attributes=none
kernel_config=CONFIG_I2C_CHARDEV is not set
dmesg_device_id=not_logged
direct_i2c_read=not_attempted
write_operations=none

The bounded SSH inventory also confirmed both drivers remain bound and the
FUSB301 interrupt path is present, but it cannot recover register 0x01 without
loading a character-device interface or changing the vendor kernel. Loading
or installing an ad-hoc I2C tool was intentionally not done: the vendor driver
owns both addresses, and the physical port/VBUS switch mapping is unresolved.

The public onsemi datasheets identify the reset Device ID as 0x12 for both
FUSB301 and FUSB301A. That is a source/datasheet expectation, not a live
readback result for this board. References: https://www.onsemi.com/download/data-sheet/pdf/fusb301-d.pdf and https://www.onsemi.com/download/data-sheet/pdf/fusb301a-d.pdf
