The vendor scan drives the selected P1 column low and the other columns high;
it interprets a low P0 row bit as a press and a high bit as a release. This is
not the default polarity/state policy of the current generic matrix candidate.

Linux 7.1.3 exposes the matching consumer-level controls as `gpio-activelow`
and `drive-inactive-cols`. With those properties, the generic matrix driver
can drive the vendor-equivalent active-low columns and report active-low rows
without cloning the vendor AW9523 silicon driver. The current disabled patch
0054 omits both properties, so it is only a topology/keymap candidate and is
not yet an electrically equivalent scan description.

The vendor's 1 ms delayed IRQ work and 100 Hz retry timer are policy evidence,
not a reason to copy its polling/suppression logic. Parent EINT polarity,
GPIO58 reset polarity, row/column wiring, debounce, rollover, wake, and the
effect of the proposed polarity properties remain hardware gates.
