Packaged modules include generic `btmtk.ko`, `hci_uart.ko`, GNSS core/serial/
MTK-serial modules, and `mt76`/Connac/MT7921 modules. `btmtkuart.ko` and
`btmtksdio.ko` are absent, and no vendor WMT/BTIF/CONSYS/Wi-Fi transport is
packaged. The `mt76` objects target other MediaTek families and are not proof
of a Gemini MAC match.

Device-tree boundary
--------------------

The packaged Gemini DTB has one retained `consys-reserve-memory` node:

  compatible = "mediatek,consys-reserve-memory";
  no-map; size = 0x200000; alignment = 0x200000;
  alloc-ranges = 0x40000000..0xc0000000

It has zero active `consys`, `wifi`, `btif`, `gps`, `gps_emi`, STP, or WMT
transport nodes. The board patch adds only the reserved-memory declaration.
This preserves firmware-owned space without exposing an unverified transport.

Source/runtime interpretation
-----------------------------

The source audit confirms the vendor transport discrepancy: the observed
legacy SDIO path uses function-2 IDs `0x6628/0x6630/0x6632`, a four-byte header,
512-byte blocks, and a 2080-byte FIFO, while Linux `btmtksdio` targets newer
IDs with a five-byte header and 256-byte blocks. The active live path also has
MT6797 BTIF DMA windows at `0x1100c000`, custom WMT firmware loading, CONSYS
supplies/clocks, and BGF/WDT IRQ ownership. The userspace audit confirms that
`wmt_launcher`, `wmt_loader`, `mtk_agpsd`, and Wi-Fi factory tools use private
WMT/BTIF/GNSS character/ioctl and firmware boundaries.

Decision
--------

The package can provide standard HCI/STP, cfg80211/mac80211, SDIO, firmware-
helper, and GNSS framework code, but it cannot bind the Gemini connectivity
hardware. A future implementation needs a new MT6797 CONSYS owner and BTIF or
legacy-SDIO transport, a firmware/retained-memory contract, and standard HCI,
cfg80211, and GNSS interfaces above those transports. Do not reuse `mt76`,
`btmtksdio`, or `gnss-mtk` by compatible-string similarity, and do not expose
the vendor `/dev/stpwmt`, `/dev/wmtWifi`, or `/dev/stpgps` ABI as mainline UAPI.
