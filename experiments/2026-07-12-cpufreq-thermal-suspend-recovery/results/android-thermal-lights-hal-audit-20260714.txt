# Android thermal and lights HAL ELF audit — 2026-07-14

## Provenance

- Inputs: private extracted `system/vendor/lib64/hw/thermal.mt6797.so` and
  `system/vendor/lib64/hw/lights.mt6797.so`.
- SHA-256: thermal
  `cf56b41a7e9cd5723e0834fe658f17a82b97eb9424b42ea6e9ea2821662403b5`;
  lights
  `a95fda097300eac1f3186bc361f244f5e4ebd6e7e4e798da71ae0e44b4ccac56`.
- Analysis: Linux VM Radare2 5.5.0 import/string inspection; static only.
- No vendor binary was executed and no device state was changed.

## Thermal HAL observations

- Imports are limited to libc file/parsing helpers (`fopen`, `getline`,
  `fscanf`, `sscanf`, `fclose`, `free`, `strerror`, and related checks) plus
  Android logging. No write, ioctl, or device-driver API is imported.
- It names the vendor procfs readers `/proc/mtktz/mtktscpu`,
  `/proc/mtktz/mtktsbattery`, and `/proc/mtktz/mtktsAP`, plus `/proc/stat` and
  CPU-online information.
- This is a read-only Android compatibility consumer of the vendor thermal
  ABI; it is not a thermal-controller implementation or a source for Linux
  trip values.

## Lights HAL observations

- The HAL imports `open` and checked writes and names standard LED-class paths:
  `/sys/class/leds/red/brightness`, `/sys/class/leds/green/brightness`,
  `/sys/class/leds/blue/brightness`, and their `trigger`, `delay_on`, and
  `delay_off` attributes.
- It also names `/sys/class/leds/jogball-backlight/brightness`,
  `lcd-backlight/brightness`, `keyboard-backlight/brightness`, and
  `button-backlight/brightness`. These are ABI strings, not proof that every
  LED or backlight is populated on this unit.
- The `set_led_state` and `write_int` strings show RGB pattern/timing and
  integer brightness writes through the standard LED sysfs surface.

## Mainline implication

The Android thermal HAL should not be ported: expose validated MT6797 thermal
and AUXADC data through Linux thermal/IIO providers, with conservative trips.
The lights HAL confirms that board policy can sit above ordinary LED and
backlight class devices; mainline should implement those providers and avoid a
vendor `/proc` or Android HAL ABI. Runtime ownership and exact LED population
still require live evidence.
