Live identity and ownership
---------------------------

The read-only PMIC collector was rerun on `gemini@192.168.1.50` using the
audited single-token `pmic_access` read path. It returned HWCID `0x5140` and
SWCID `0x5120`, confirming MT6351 E2. It did not issue a PMIC write, toggle a
rail, set an alarm, or change an interrupt mask.

The current vendor image still binds `mt-pmic` and `mt-rtc`. The pwrap IRQ
line was idle in this snapshot, while the downstream `pmic-eint` line showed
activity; the zero pwrap counter is not evidence that the wrapper is absent.
The 26 MHz `pmicspi_sel`, `infra_pmic_ap`, `infra_pmic_md`, and
`infra_pmic_conn` clock rates remain visible. Existing vendor regulator users
include storage rails `vemc_3v3`, `vmch`, and `vmc`, plus connectivity rails
`vcn18`, `vcn28`, `vcn33_bt`, and `vcn33_wifi`.

The allowlisted buck control readback decodes to the same active selectors as
the prior capture: VCORE 0.850 V, VGPU 1.21875 V, VMODEM 1.000 V, VMD1
0.850 V, VSRAM_MD 1.100 V, VS1 1.000 V, VS2 0.675 V, VPA disabled at the
0.600 V floor, and VSRAM_PROC 1.100 V. These are selector-derived transient
observations, not electrical measurements or safe OPP limits. The vendor
`dump_ldo_status` direct-selector display still differs from hardware-selected
ON-bank values for hardware-controlled bucks; mainline must retain the active
selector ownership logic.

-----------------------

The authoritative current DTB contains:

Source and descriptor decisions
-------------------------------

The current probe-safety audit confirms that pwrap probe is stateful: it
enables clocks, may reset/reinitialize the serial bus, and writes watchdog,
timer, dynamic-clock, and interrupt-enable registers. MT6351 MFD probe masks
all four PMIC interrupt-enable banks before creating its IRQ domain. The
regulator probe reads revision/VSEL state without enabling a rail; key and RTC
operations can write later. Therefore the current DT is an active boot-risk
boundary, not a passive description.

The vendor-to-mainline descriptor extractor validates all 39 unique rails
(nine bucks plus 30 unique LDO controls), explicitly excluding the duplicate
`vldo28_0` and invalid vendor `vsram_proc` LDO artifacts. MT6351 E2 is accepted;
the unvalidated E3 revision remains rejected. Existing Linux pwrap, MFD,
regulator, key, and RTC frameworks are reusable with MT6797/MT6351 data, but
runtime ownership, interrupt masking, constraints, coupling, ramp behavior,
and suspend/OPP policy still require controlled hardware validation.

runtime_mainline_probe=not_attempted
rail_control=not_attempted
rtc_write_or_alarm=not_attempted
hardware_write=none
