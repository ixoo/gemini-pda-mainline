## Resource contract

The flattened vendor tree describes SCPSYS resources at `0x10001000`,
`0x10006000`, and `0x10201000`; the vendor clock-gate source uses them as
infracfg, SPM, and infra bases respectively. Linux 7.1.3's generic SCPSYS
driver maps `0x10006000` as its power-control resource and resolves
`0x10001000` through the `infracfg` syscon, which is the correct equivalent
for its generic API.

Vendor MFG control registers are `0x334`, `0x338`, `0x340`, `0x344`, `0x348`,
and `0x34c`, with the shared SRAM control/ack register at `0x33c`. Status bits
are MFG async 13, MFG 12, and cores 0--3 at 11, 10, 9, and 8.

## SPM firmware boundary

The vendor debugfs endpoint reports PCM images named `pcm_suspend`,
`pcm_suspend_by_mp1`, `pcm_vcorefs_lpm`, `pcm_vcorefs_hpm`,
`pcm_vcorefs_ultra`, `pcm_sodi`, `pcm_sodi_by_mp1`, `pcm_deepidle`, and
`pcm_deepidle_by_mp1`. Only names and sizes were retained in private raw
capture; no image contents are included here.

## Interpretation

Panfrost can reuse the Midgard GPU core model, but Linux 7.1.3 lacks the
MT6797 MFG clock provider and the MFG/core SCPSYS data. The generic providers
can be extended with the separate SRAM register offsets; no new GPU silicon
driver is implied by this platform gap.
