Linux 7.1.3 coverage audit

Reusable upstream drivers and bindings:
- BMI160: drivers/iio/imu/bmi160/; binding
  Documentation/devicetree/bindings/iio/imu/bosch,bmi160.yaml; Kconfig
  symbols BMI160_I2C and BMI160_SPI; OF compatible bosch,bmi160. Binding
  supports optional INT1/INT2, vdd/vddio supplies, and mount-matrix.
- BMP280: drivers/iio/pressure/bmp280-i2c.c and bmp280-core.c; binding
  Documentation/devicetree/bindings/iio/pressure/bmp085.yaml; Kconfig
  BMP280_I2C; OF compatible bosch,bmp280.
- HTS221: drivers/iio/humidity/hts221_i2c.c and hts221_core.c; binding
  Documentation/devicetree/bindings/iio/humidity/st,hts221.yaml; Kconfig
  HTS221_I2C; OF compatible st,hts221.
- LSM6DS3 family: drivers/iio/imu/st_lsm6dsx/; Kconfig IIO_ST_LSM6DSX.
  This is only an alternate source candidate on the Gemini, not a confirmed
  live part.
- MMC35240: drivers/iio/magnetometer/mmc35240.c; Kconfig MMC35240. It is the
  closest upstream MEMSIC implementation to the vendor MMC3530 candidate, but
  the register/ID equivalence is not established.
- STK3310 family: drivers/iio/light/stk3310.c; binding
  Documentation/devicetree/bindings/iio/light/stk33xx.yaml; Kconfig STK3310;
  OF compatibles sensortek,stk3013, sensortek,stk3310, sensortek,stk3311, and
  sensortek,stk3335. Its register map overlaps the vendor STK3X1X contract,
  but it recognizes explicit product IDs and has no generic stk3x1x compatible.

No Linux 7.1.3 driver or DT binding directly matched the vendor name:
- stk3x1x (the STK3310-family driver is a reuse candidate; exact product ID
  and revision are not yet captured)
- mmc3530
- mediatek,stk3x1x
- mediatek,mmc3530
- mediatek,bmi160_acc
- mediatek,gyro_bmi160
- mediatek,gsensor_bmi160
- mediatek,humidity
- mediatek,barometer

The vendor STK3X1X header and reconstructed-kernel disassembly show the same
8-bit register protocol used by the upstream STK3310-family driver: state and
control at 0x00-0x05, thresholds at 0x06-0x0d, flags at 0x10, data at
0x11-0x14, and product ID at 0x3e. The vendor accepts product-ID high nibbles
0x10/0x20/0x30, broader than the upstream explicit ID list. See
[stk3310-reuse-audit.txt](stk3310-reuse-audit.txt); do not add a generic
compatible or enable a board node until a safe live ID/revision read is
available.

The Linux BMI160 core reads the Bosch chip ID and recognizes `0xd1` and
`0xd3`, enables vdd/vddio supplies, and exposes standard
accelerometer/gyroscope IIO channels. In 7.1.3 an unrecognized ID is warned
about but does not abort chip initialization, so a successful probe is not
itself an identity proof. A Gemini DT node must use the standard compatible and
provide only verified board resources; a vendor-compatible alias alone would
not make the driver probe. The vendor probe/address and ID comparison details
are in [vendor-imu-probe.txt](vendor-imu-probe.txt).
