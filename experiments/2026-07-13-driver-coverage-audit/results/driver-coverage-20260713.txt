Static correlation:

  UART: live `mtk-uart`; `CONFIG_SERIAL_8250_MT6577=y`,
  `CONFIG_MTK_UART_APDMA=m`, `mtk8250_probe` linked in Image. Reuse
  `8250_mtk` PIO for the first console; vendor AP-DMA remains deferred.

  MSDC: live `mtk-msdc`; `CONFIG_MMC_MTK=y`, `msdc_drv_probe` linked in
  Image. Reuse `mtk-sd` with the local MT6797 register/quirk data.

  Watchdog: live `mtk-wdt`; `CONFIG_MEDIATEK_WATCHDOG=y`,
  `mtk_wdt_probe` linked in Image. Reuse the upstream watchdog protocol with
  the source-backed Gemini IRQ; runtime testing needs recovery protection.

  Pinctrl/EINT: live `mediatek-mt6797-pinctrl`; `CONFIG_PINCTRL_MT6797=y`.
  The generic framework is linked in Image and the local series supplies the
  decoded MT6797 EINT data. IRQ delivery and wake remain hardware gates.

  PMIC: the live pwrap node has no sysfs driver link in this view, while
  `CONFIG_MTK_PMIC_WRAP=y`, `CONFIG_MFD_MT6397=y`, and
  `CONFIG_REGULATOR_MT6351=y` link their probe paths into Image. This is a
  standard MFD/regulator/RTC reuse boundary with consumers deliberately gated;
  absence of a sysfs link is not treated as proof that the vendor pwrap is
  inactive.

  USB: live vendor USB11/USB3 links are `musb11_dts` and `musb-mtu3d`, with
  the xHCI child unbound. Linux MUSB, MTU3, and xHCI probe paths are linked in
  Image. Generic cores plus local MT6797 glue are the reuse boundary; gadget
  only is the first runtime gate.

  Display/audio/thermal: `CONFIG_DRM_MEDIATEK=m`,
  `CONFIG_SND_SOC_MT6797=m`, and `CONFIG_MTK_THERMAL=m`; these paths are not
  available in the authoritative Image; optional modules are packaged
  separately but no rootfs loads them in this evidence set.
  They remain build-only until a rootfs/modules package, boot evidence, and
  display/audio/thermal ownership or calibration are established.

  M4U/SMI/CMDQ: live vendor ownership is `m4u` and `mtk_cmdq`; Linux IOMMU,
  SMI, CMDQ, and MMSYS providers are built in, with local MT6797 data. Attach
  one verified DMA consumer at a time rather than enabling the entire vendor
  multimedia graph.

  CONSYS/CCCI/camera: live vendor ownership includes `mtk_wmt`, `cldma_modem`,
  and `camera-isp`. These are not drop-in Linux interfaces; firmware ownership,
  shared memory, transport ABI, and camera pipeline contracts remain separate
  investigations.
